This demo app will give you a measure of how trustworthy an article is, based on similarities with a large collection of other articles.

This is intended as a proof of concept, so the unreliability rating is defiitely not intended to be definitive. Rather, the higher the rating â€” on a scale up to 100, the greater the chance that the content is not reliable and you should be cautious about taking it at face value.

This is a rather basic and limited interface that will not work well or perhaps at all with anything but plain text. Copy and paste an article, or at least several paragraphs, into the text area below and click the submit button. The algorithm removes at least the first and last sentences, and anything in parentheses or brackets. Additionally, it will reduce quotations of any length to a single sentence comprising one or more instances of the word "quote" so that content that is not directly part of the article itself is not used to evaluate the article. An article with an uneven number of (double) quotation marks will not be evaluated, since the algorithm could not accurately determine which words were inside of quotations and which outside. What is left must be at least 10 sentences long, or the algorithm will not perform an evaluation. This is based on the assumption that articles with too few sentences/words do not provide enough of a basis for a reasonable determination of reliability.
